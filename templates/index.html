About the System

The CCS Sit-in Monitoring System is developed for the College of Computer Studies at the University of Cebu. It provides a streamlined way for students and faculty to track laboratory sit-in sessions throughout the semester.

The system ensures that students are aware of their sit-in usage and helps administrators efficiently manage laboratory access and attendance records.

  • ✔ Track sit-in sessions per semester
  • ✔ Easy student registration and login
  • ✔ Real-time monitoring for faculty and staff
  • ✔ Secure and organized record-keeping

📜 Lab Rules & Regulations

All students must comply with the following rules during sit-in sessions.

The CCS Computer Laboratories are shared academic resources. To maintain a productive and respectful environment for all users, the following rules are strictly enforced. Violations may result in the suspension of sit-in privileges.

  • 1Students must log in to the Sit-in Monitoring System before using any laboratory computer. Unauthorized use without a logged session is not permitted.
  • 2Maintain silence and proper decorum at all times. Mobile phones must be set to silent mode. Loud conversations and disruptive behavior are not allowed.
  • 3Playing computer games, watching non-academic videos, or accessing social media is strictly prohibited during sit-in sessions.
  • 4Downloading, installing, or uninstalling any software is not allowed without prior permission from the laboratory-in-charge.
  • 5Do not alter system settings, desktop configurations, or any computer files that do not belong to your work.
  • 6Food and drinks are strictly prohibited inside the laboratory at all times.
  • 7Keep the laboratory clean and orderly. Place all bags and personal belongings in the designated area, not on the computer tables.
  • 8Report any damaged equipment, hardware malfunctions, or technical issues immediately to the laboratory-in-charge.
  • 9Students must log out of the system and properly shut down the computer before leaving. Inform the laboratory-in-charge when your session ends.
  • 10Respect the property of others. Accessing another student's files, accounts, or personal data is a serious violation subject to disciplinary action.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

Got questions? Find quick answers below.

A sit-in session is a recorded instance of a student using the CCS computer laboratory outside of their regular class schedule. Each visit is logged in the system and counted against your semester session limit.
CCS students (BSIT and BSCS) are allowed 30 sit-in sessions per semester. Students from all other courses are allowed 15 sit-in sessions per semester. Sessions reset at the start of each new semester.
Click the Register button in the top navigation bar. Fill in your ID number, name, course, year level, email, and password. Once submitted, your account will be active and you can log in immediately.
Go to the Login page and click "Forgot Password?". Enter your ID number and registered email address. A password reset link will be sent to your email. Follow the link to set a new password.

Your session is deducted from your remaining count when the laboratory-in-charge logs you out of the system, not when you enter. Make sure to inform the in-charge before leaving so your session is properly ended.
After logging in to your student account, go to your Dashboard. Your remaining session count is displayed in the Student Information panel on the home tab.
